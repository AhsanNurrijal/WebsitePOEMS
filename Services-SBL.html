Securities Borrowing and Lending

Take Advantage of a Bear Market!


Have you been short-changed lately?


Life is full of surprises and challenges – ups and down, but why should that keep you out of the market?


Now with a Securities Borrowing and Lending Account (SBL) from Phillip Sekuritas Indonesia, you have the advantage even in a market or economic downturn.


Short-selling is a classic strategy to make the best of a declining market when the momentum of stocks or overall trend of the stock market is poor.


Don’t let the market short-change you!


What is SBL (Securities Borrowing and Lending) ?

Securities Borrowing and Lending (SBL) is a system in which traders borrow shares that they do not already own, or lend the stocks that they own but do not intend to sell immediately.


What is the benefit of SBL?

  1. Potential gains during bearish market.
  2. Increase transaction earnings.
  3. Added-values for lenders as owners of the borrowed stocks.

Securities Borrowing and Lending Account Instruction

Why Use Margin?

  1. Trading – For trading convenience, you can trade through your ordinary share trading accounts, either online or through your Dealer, Remisier, or Financial Advisor.
  2. Settlement – Short contract will be amended and settled through the SBL Account. Borrowed shares will be delivered on Due Date.
  3. Acceptable Collateral – Collateral must be deposited before performing a short trade. Acceptable collateral is either cash or securities prescribed by Phillip Sekuritas Indonesia. All acceptable securities will be assigned a collateralized value.
  4. Borrowing of Securities - The list of available shares and quantity available for scrip borrowing may differ from day to day. An updated list can be obtained from your Dealer, Remisier, or Financial Advisor.
  5. Returning of Securities - A buy-back through the SBL account does not automatically translate into a return of shares. You will have to return the borrowed shares either online or by informing your Dealer, Remisier, or Financial Advisor.
  6. Borrowed shares must be returned before the book closure date for dividends, rights, bonus and other entitlements.
  7. If acceptable collateral has not been received, we will not borrow any share on your behalf.
